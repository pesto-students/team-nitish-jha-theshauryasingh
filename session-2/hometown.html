Faridabad, Haryana, India

Location

It is located 284 kilometres south of the Haryana's capital Chandigarh.
It is in south of Delhi, East of Gurugram, and west of Noida.
The river Yamuna forms the eastern district boundary with Uttar Pradesh.

About

Faridabad is named after its founder, Shaikh Farid, the treasurer of Jahangir who built a town here.
Faridabad is the most populous city in the Indian state of Haryana and a part of Delhi National Capital Region.
It is one of the major satellite cities around Delhi

Weather

The weather of Faridabad is an overlap between monsoon-influenced humid subtropical with high variation between summer and winter temperatures and precipitation.
Summer starts in early April and peaks in late May or early June, with occasional heat waves. The monsoon starts in late June and lasts until mid-September.
The monsoons recede in late September, and the post-monsoon season continues till late October, with average temperatures slides down for atleast 5 degrees.
Winter starts in November and peaks in January. Faridabad's proximity to the Himalayas results in cold waves leading to lower apparent temperature due to wind chill.
Faridabad is notorious for its heavy fogs and haze during the winter season. Winter ends by the first week of March.

Population

As per 2011 census, Faridabad had a population of 1,414,050 out of which there were 754,542 males and 659,508 females
